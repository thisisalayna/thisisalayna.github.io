Pit bulls pumped up with performance-boosting drugs among 28 dogs found in raids on dog-fighting ring in Manhattan, Long Island

By Graham Rayman and Larry McShane
New York Daily News |
Oct 10, 2019 | 3:43 PM

Authorities recovered 28 dogs, including a number of pit bulls pumped up with performance-enhancing drugs, in coordinated Thursday raids targeting a dog-fighting ring, the NYPD and ASPCA confirmed.

Investigators descended on a home in Uniondale, L.I., and on a location on 162nd St. in Washington Heights, authorities said. A third Manhattan location was reportedly involved as well in the operation that stretched from the suburbs to the city, sources indicated.

“Tremendous work by the NYPD’s Animal Cruelty Investigation Squad & the Grand Larceny Division — with our partners in the ASPCA — in rescuing 28 dogs as part of a large-scale, ongoing animal cruelty investigation. Impressive and important work. Well done!” tweeted NYPD Chief of Detective Dermot Shea about the takedown.

According to neighbors, authorities in hazmat suits swarmed a two-story home on Cooper Court in Uniondale around 10 a.m., leading more than a dozen animals out of the residence. Reports indicated one man was arrested, and weapons were also recovered inside the house.

The ASPCA released a statement confirming it was working with the NYPD “in the removal, forensic evidence collection, medical care and sheltering” of the recovered dogs.
